Classify

This feature will classify or detect your used voice register on your uploaded recordings. Only 4 voice registers will be detected: Vocal Fry, Chest Voice, Head Voice and Falsetto. This will allow you to track and practice your desired voice register using your own recordings.

Learn more about voice registers...


Vocal Fry

Vocal fry is the lowest register (tone) of your voice characterized by its deep, creaky, breathy sound.


Vocal Fry Sample Video

Chest Voice

The term “chest voice” refers to the register in which most people speak and sing low to medium-high pitches.


Chest Voice Sample Video

Head Voice

A register above chest voice, refers to the sort of singing voice that gives a ringing feeling in the singer's head.


Head Voice Sample Video



Falsetto

Falsetto is a mode of singing that sounds breathy, flutey and hollow. It’s usually found in the upper registers of male and female singers.


Falsetto Sample Video

Mixed Voice

Mixed voice is when you combine your head voice and chest voice to create an even singing tone from the bottom to the top of your voice.


Mixed Voice Sample Video

Whistle Register

Whistle Register is the highest vocal register created by the isolated vibration of the anterior section of the vocal cords.

ABOUT US AND VREGI APP

Voice register is another method or notion in singing voice that beginners should be aware of. This web application aims to implement a classification model that can distinguish the spectral difference of the four voice registers: Vocal Fry, Chest Voice, Head Voice and Falsetto where it can assist users in understanding how to use them. Also, it has video tutorials and voice exercises on each voice register made by a vocal coach named "Freya Casey" (Direct to her youtube page).


Machine Learning was utilized to the development of the web application. A classification model was trained and built by train and test datasets to generate and analyze the relationship between the attributes of the dataset and the resulting model can be used to classify or predict a user's preference for any unseen new items or data.
